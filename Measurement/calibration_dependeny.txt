Q Document why raw (distorted) images produce incorrect measurements. 


Raw (distorted) images give wrong measurements because the camera lens bends the image, 
so pixels don’t stay in a straight, equal scale. Due to radial distortion, straight lines 
can look slightly curved (bulged in or out), and tangential distortion shifts parts of the 
image unevenly because the lens is not perfectly aligned. Because of this, the scale is not 
constant across the image — meaning the same number of pixels can represent different real-world 
distances depending on where the object is in the frame. So if we directly convert pixels to mm, 
the values become inconsistent and error increases, especially near the edges of the image. Using 
undistortion fixes this by correcting the warped image using the camera calibration parameters, so 
the geometry becomes more stable and measurement becomes reliable.
